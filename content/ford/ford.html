FORD RIVER ROUGE

Dearborn, Michigan
1999 - 2001

Henry Ford built the American dream of a manufacturing facility where coal came in one end and a finished car out the other. Generations toiled at this 1200-acre plant on the River Rouge, where today hundreds of Mustangs are churned out every day. William Clay Ford Jr. envisions the opportunity to transform this industrial icon of the twentieth century into a model of sustainable industry for the twenty-first century. Ambitious environmental initiatives are employed with emerging technologies forming a new landscape of production, manufacturing vehicles along with clean water, air and soil. Storm water management systems include parking lots surfaced with porous paving made from slag, a by-product of steel-making, and industrial strength hedgerow swales return filtered water to the River Rouge. Phytoremediation fields, developed in conjunction with Michigan State University researchers, extract contaminants from the soil of the former historic Coke and By-Product Operations, while also contributing to scientific knowledge in the field of remediation technologies and offering visible signs of regeneration. The Miller Road Corridor creates a public industrial heritage boulevard and welcoming entry for workers and families.
